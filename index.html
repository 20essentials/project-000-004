SODA STEREO

The love for music

Soda Stereo was an iconic Argentine rock band formed in 1982 by Gustavo Cerati, Zeta Bosio and Charly Alberti. With their innovative style and poetic lyrics, they became pioneers of rock in Spanish. They fused rock with elements of new wave and pop, creating a unique sound that resonated throughout Latin America. Their legacy includes iconic albums such as "Signos" and "Canción Animal", as well as timeless hits such as "De Música Ligera". Soda Stereo transcended cultural and generational boundaries, leaving an indelible mark on the history of Spanish-language music.
